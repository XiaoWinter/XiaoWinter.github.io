## Variables[ ˈverɪəbl]

MATHEMATICS As a science commenced[kəˈmenst ] when first someone, probably a Greek, proved propositions about any things or about some things , without specification[ˌspesɪfɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n ] of definite[ ˈdefɪnət ] particular[pərˈtɪkjələr] things. These propositions[命题] were first enunciated[ ɪˈnʌnsiːeɪtɪd] by the Greeks for geometry; and , accordingly[ əˈkɔːrdɪŋli], geometry was the great Greek mathematical science .After the rise of geometry centuries[ˈsentʃəriz] passed away before algebra[ˈældʒɪbrə] made a really effective[ ɪˈfektɪv] start, despite[dɪˈspaɪt] some faint[feɪnt] anticipations[ænˌtɪsɪˈpeɪʃ(ə)ns ] by the later Greek mathematicians.

数学作为一门科学始于某人（可能是希腊人）首次证明关于任何事物或某些事物的命题，而不需要具体指定某个确定的事物。这些命题最初由希腊人用于几何学，并且，相应地，几何学成为了古希腊重要的数学科学。在几何学兴起后的几个世纪里，尽管一些后来的希腊数学家略有预期，但直到代数真正开始起步之前，还过了很长时间。
<!--more-->
The ideas of any and of some are introduced into algebra by the use of letters ,instead of the definite numbers of arithmetic[əˈrɪθmətɪk]. Thus, instead of saying that 2+3=3+2, in algebra we generalize[ˈdʒen(ə)rəlaɪz] and say that , if x and y stand for any two numbers, then $x+y=y+x$. Again , in the place[pleɪs] of saying that 3>2, we generalize and say that if x be any number there exists some number (or numbers ) y such that $y>x$. We may remark in passing[ˈpæsɪŋ] that this latter[ˈlætər] assumption[ əˈsʌmpʃ(ə)n ]—for when put in its strict[strɪkt] ultimate[ˈʌltɪmət] form it is an assumption[əˈsʌmpʃ(ə)n] — is of vital[ˈvaɪt(ə)l ] importance , both to philosophy[fəˈlɑːsəfi ] and to mathematics; for by it the notion[ˈnoʊʃ(ə)n] of infinity is introduced. Perhaps it required the introduction of the arabic[ˈærəbɪk] numerals[ˈnjʊmərəl], by which the use of letters as standing for definite numbers has been completely discarded[dɪˈskɑːrdɪd ] in mathematics , in order to suggest to mathematicians the technical convenience[ kənˈviːniəns ] of the use of letters for thr ideas of any number and some number. The Romans would have stated[ ˈsteɪtɪd ] the number of the year in which this is written in the form MDCCCCX, whereas[ˌwerˈæz ] we write it 1910, thus leaving the letters for the other usage[ˈjuːsɪdʒ] .But this is merely[ ˈmɪrli ] a speculation[ˌspekjuˈleɪʃ(ə)n]. After the rise of algebra[ ˈældʒɪbrə ] the differential[ˌdɪfəˈrenʃ(ə)l] calculus[ ˈkælkjələs ] was invented by Newton and Leibniz, and then a pause[ pɔːz] in the progress of the philosophy of mathematical thought occurred[əˈkɜːrd ] so far as these notions are concerned[ kənˈsɜːrnd] ; and it was not till[tɪl] within the last few years that it has been realized how fundamental[ ˌfʌndəˈment(ə)l ] $any$ and $some$ are to the very nature of mathematics ,with the result of opening out still[ stɪl] further[ˈfɜːrðər] subjects for mathematical exploration[ˌekspləˈreɪʃ(ə)n].

代数中引入了任意数和某些数的概念，通过使用字母，而不是算术中的确定数字。因此，在代数中，我们不是说2+3=3+2，而是概括地说，如果 x 和 y 代表任意两个数字，则 $x+y=y+x$。同样，在说3>2的地方，我们概括地说，如果 x 是任意数字，则存在某个数（或数）y，使得$y>x$。我们顺便提一下，这后一种假设，严格来说，是一个假设，它对哲学和数学都至关重要；因为通过它，无限的概念被引入。也许，需要引入阿拉伯数字，通过这种方式，在数学中完全放弃了将字母作为确定数字的用法，以向数学家们暗示使用字母表示任何数字和某些数字的技术便利。罗马人会用MDCCCCX表示写这篇文章的年份，而我们写作1910，因此留下了字母供其他用途。但这只是一种猜测。代数兴起后，牛顿和莱布尼茨发明了微积分，数学思想的哲学进展在这些概念方面出现了停顿；直到最近几年才意识到$any$和$some$对数学的本质有多么重要，这导致进一步开拓了数学探索的课题。

Let us now make some simple algebraic[ ˌældʒɪˈbreɪɪk] statements[ˈsteɪtmənts], with the object of understanding exactly[ɪɡˈzæktli ] how these fundamental ideas occur[əˈkɜːr].

现在让我们做一些简单的代数陈述，目的是确切地理解这些基本概念是如何产生的。

(1) For any number x , x+2=2+x;

(2) For some number x,x+2=3;

(3) For some number x, x+2>3;

The first point to notice is the possibilities[ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti] contained[ kənˈteɪnd] in the meaning of $some$, as here used. Since x+2=2+x for $any$ number x, it is true for $some$ number x. Thus, as here used, $any$ implies[ɪmˈplaɪz] $some$ and $some$ does not exclude[ ɪkˈskluːd] $any$. Again, in the second example, there is , in fact , only one number x, such as x+2=3, namely[ˈneɪmli] only the number 1. Thus the $some$ may be that one number only. But in the third example, any number x which is greater than 1 gives x+2>3. Hence[hens] there are an infinite number of numbers which answer to the some number in this case. Thus $some$ may be anything between $any$ and $one only$, including both these limiting[ˈlɪmɪtɪŋ] cases.

第一个要注意的是，在这里使用的$some$一词所包含的可能性。由于对于任意数字x，x+2=2+x成立，因此对于某个数字x也成立。因此，如此使用时，$any$ 意味着$some$，而$some$并不排除 $any$。再次，在第二个例子中，实际上只有一个数字x，比如x+2=3，只有数字1。因此，$some$可能只是那一个数字。但在第三个例子中，大于1的任意数字x都满足x+2>3。因此，在这种情况下，有无穷多个数字与$some$对应。因此，$some$可能是介于$any$和$one only$”之间的任何东西，包括这两种极限情况。

It is natural[ˈnætʃ(ə)rəl] to supersede[ˌsuːpərˈsiːd] the statements (2) and (3) by the questions:

这两个陈述(2)和(3)很自然地被以下问题取代：

(2') For what number x is x+2=3;

(3') For what numbers x is x+2>3.

Considering (2'), x+2=3 is equation[ɪˈkweɪʒ(ə)n], and it is easy to see that its solution[ səˈluːʃ(ə)n] is x=3-2=1. When we have asked the question implied[ɪmˈplaɪd] in the statement of the equation  x+2=3, x is called the unknown. The object of the solution of the equation is the determination[dɪˌtɜːrmɪˈneɪʃ(ə)n] of the unknown. Equations are of great importance in mathematics , and it seems as though[ðoʊ] (2') exemplified[ ɪɡˈzemplɪfaɪ] a much more thoroughgoing[ˌθɜːroʊˈɡoʊɪŋ] and fundamental idea than the original[ əˈrɪdʒən(ə)l] statement (2).This, however, is a complete mistake.The idea of the undetermined[ˌʌndɪˈtɜːrmɪnd]  'variable'[ˈveriəb(ə)l ] as occurring[əˈkɜːrɪŋ] in the use of $some$ or $any$ is the really important one in mathematics; that of the 'unknown' in an equation, which is to be solved as quickly as possible, is only of subordinate[səˈbɔːrdɪnət] use, though of course it is very important.  One of the causes of the apparent[ əˈpærənt] triviality[ˌtrɪviˈæləti] of much of elementary algebra is the preoccupation[ priˌɑːkjuˈpeɪʃn] the text-books with the solution of equations. The same remark[ rɪˈmɑːrk] applies[ əˈplaɪz] to the solution of the inequality[ˌɪnɪˈkwɑːləti] (3') as compared to the original statement(3).

考虑方程(2')，x+2=3是一个方程，很容易看出它的解是x=3-2=1。当我们提出了在方程x+2=3的陈述中隐含的问题时，x被称为未知数。解方程的目的是确定未知数的值。方程在数学中具有重要性，似乎(2')展示了比原始陈述(2)更为彻底和基础的想法。然而，这是一个完全错误的观点。在数学中，作为在使用$some$或$any$中出现的未确定的“变量”的概念才是真正重要的；而在方程中，需要尽快解决的“未知数”的概念只是次要的，尽管它当然也是非常重要的。初等代数中许多问题显得琐碎的一个原因是教科书过于关注解方程式。同样的评论适用于不等式(3')的解决方法，与原始陈述(3)相比。

But the majority[məˈdʒɔːrəti] of interesting formulae[ˈfɔːrmjəliː] , especially[ ɪˈspeʃəli ] when the idea of some is present, involve more than one variable. For example, the consideration of the pairs of numbers x and y (fractional[ ˈfrækʃən(ə)l] or integral[ˈɪntɪɡrəl ]) which satisfy x+y=1 involves the idea of two correlatedˈkɔːrəleɪtɪd variables, x and y. When two variables are present[ˈprez(ə)nt] the same two main types of statement occur[ əˈkɜːr ]. For example, (1) for any pair of numbers , x and y, $x+y=y+x$, and (2) for some pairs of numbers ,x and y, x+y=1.

但是，大多数有趣的公式，特别是当涉及某个概念时，通常包含多个变量。例如，考虑满足 x+y=1 的数字对 x 和 y（可以是分数或整数），涉及两个相关变量 x 和 y 的概念。当存在两个变量时，会出现相同的两种主要类型的陈述。例如，(1) 对于任意一对数字 x 和 y，有 $x+y=y+x$；(2) 对于某些数字对 x 和 y，有 x+y=1。

The second type of statement invites[ˈɪnvaɪts ] consideration of the aggregate[ˈæɡrɪɡət] of pairs of numbers which are bound[ baʊnd] together by some fixed relation—in the case given, by the relation x+y=1. One use of formulae[ˈfɔːrmjəliː] of the first type, true for any pair of numbers , is that by them formulae of the second type can be thrown into an indefinite[ɪnˈdefɪnət] number of equivalent[ ɪˈkwɪvələnt] forms. For example, the relation x+y=1 is equivalent to the relations

y+x=1,(x-y)+2y=1, 6x+6y=6,

and so on. Thus a skillful mathematician uses that equivalent[ ɪˈkwɪvələnt] form of the relation under consideration which is most convenient[kənˈviːniənt] for his immediate purpose.

第二类陈述引发了对数对集合的考虑，这些数对通过某种固定关系联系在一起——在给定的例子中，通过关系 x+y=1 联系在一起。第一类公式的一个用途是，对于任意一对数字都成立的公式，可以将第二类公式转换成无限多种等价形式。例如，关系 x+y=1 等价于以下关系：

y+x=1, (x-y)+2y=1, 6x+6y=6，

等等。因此，熟练的数学家会使用最方便于其当前目的的等价形式来考虑所讨论的关系。

It is not in general true that, when a pair of terms[tɜːrmz] satisfy some fixed relation, if one of the terms is given the other is also definitely[ˈdefɪnətli ] determined. For example, when x and y satisfy $y^2 =x $ , if x=4, y can be $\pm 2$  , thus ,for any positive[ˈpɑːzətɪv] value of x there are alternative[ɔːlˈtɜːrnətɪv] values for y. Also in the relation $x+y>1$ , when either x or y is given, an indefinite[ɪnˈdefɪnət] number of values remain open for the other.

一般来说，并不是所有的情况下，当一对术语满足某一固定关系时，如果已知其中一个术语，那么另一个术语也能够确定下来。例如，当 𝑥 和 𝑦 满足  $y^2 =x $  时，如果 𝑥=4，𝑦可以是  $\pm 2$，因此，对于任何正值的 𝑥，都存在多种可能的 𝑦 值。同样，在关系 $x+y>1$中，如果已知 𝑥或 𝑦中的一个，另一个术语将存在无限多个可能的取值。

Again there is another important point to be noticed. If we restrict ourselves to positive numbers, integral or fractional , in considering the relation $x+y=1$ , then, if either $x$ or $y$ be greater than 1, there is no positive number which the other can assume[ əˈsuːm] so as to satisfy the relation. Thus the 'field' of the relation for $x$ is restricted[ rɪˈstrɪktɪd] to numbers less than 1, and similarly for the 'field' open to $y$. Again, consider integral numbers only, positive or negative , and take the relation $y^2=x$ , satisfied by pairs of such numbers. Then a whatever integral value is given to $y$ , $x$ can assume one corresponding integral value.  So the 'field' for $y$ is unrestricted among there positive or negative integers. But the 'field' for $x$  is restricted in two ways. In the first place $x$ mist be positive, and in the second place, since $y$ is to be integral, $x$ must be a perfect square. Accordingly, the 'field' of $x$ is restricted to the set of integers $1^2,2^2,3^2,4^2$ , and so on ,i.e. to $1,4,9,16$ , and so on.

再次有另一个重要的观点需要注意。如果我们将自己限制在考虑关系$x+y=1$ 中的正数，无论是整数还是分数，那么，如果  $x$  或 $y$中的任意一个大于1，那么就没有正数可以使另一个数能够满足这个关系。因此， $x$ 的关系“领域”被限制在小于1的数字， $y$的情况类似。再次，仅考虑整数，正数或负数，并取满足 $y^2=x$ 的数对。然后，无论 $y$被赋予任何整数值， $x$ 都可以假设一个对应的整数值。所以 $y$的“领域”在正整数或负整数中是不受限制的。但是 $x$ 的“领域”有两种限制。首先， $x$ 必须是正数，其次，由于  $y$ 必须是整数，所以  $x$  必须是一个完全平方数。因此， $x$ 的“领域”被限制在整数集 $1^2,2^2,3^2,4^2$，等等，即$1,4,9,16$，等等。

Draw two lines $OX$ and $OY$ at right angles[ ˈæŋɡlz ] ; let any number $x$ be represented[ ˌreprɪˈzentɪd /] by $x$ units (in any scale) of length along $OX$, any number $y$ and $y$  units (in any scale) of length along $OY$ .Thus if $OM$, along $OX$, be $x$ units in length, and $ON$ , along $OY$ , be $y$ units in length, by completing the parallelogram[ ˌpærəˈleləɡræm] $OMPN$ we find a point $P$ which corresponds[ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːnd] to the pair of numbers $x$ and $y$ . To each  point thee corresponds one pair of numbers, and to each pair of numbers there corresponds[ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːnd] one point. The pair of numbers are called the co-ordinates[koʊˈɔːrdɪneɪt ] of the point. Then the points whose co-ordinates satisfy some fixed relation can be indicated[ˈɪndɪkeɪtɪd ] in a convenient[ kənˈviːniənt] way, by drawing a line , if they all lie on a line or by shading[ ˈʃeɪdɪŋ ] an area if they are all points in the area. If the relation can be represented by an equation such as $x+y=1$ , or $y^2=x$, then the points lie on a line, which is straight[streɪt ] in the former[ˈfɔːrmər] case and curved[kɜːrvd ] in the latter. For example, considering only positive numbers, the points whose co-ordinates satisfy $x+y=1$ lie on the straight line $AB$ in Fig.1, where $OA=1$ and $OB=1$ . Thus this segment[ˈseɡmənt] of the straight[ streɪt ] line $AB$ gives a pictorial[pɪkˈtɔːriəl] representation[ˌreprɪzenˈteɪʃ(ə)n] of the properties of the relation under the restriction[ rɪˈstrɪkʃ(ə)n ] to positive numbers.

画两条相互垂直的直线 *$OX$* 和 ；让任意数 $x$ 通过 *$OX$* 方向的 $x$ 个单位长度（在任意比例尺度下）表示，任意数 $y$ 通过 $OY$ 方向的 $y$ 个单位长度（在任意比例尺度下）表示。因此，如果 $OM$ 在$OX$上的长度为 $x$ 个单位，$ON$在 $OY$ 上的长度为 $y$ 个单位，通过完成平行四边形 $OMPN$ 我们找到了一个对应于数对 $x$ 和 $y$ 的点 $P$。对于每一个点，都有一个对应的数对，而对于每一个数对，都有一个对应的点。这个数对被称为点的坐标。那些坐标满足某个固定关系的点可以通过方便的方式指示出来，例如通过画一条线，如果它们都在一条直线上，或者通过给区域涂色，如果它们都是区域内的点。如果关系可以用方程表示，比如 $x+y=1$ 或 $y^2=x$，那么这些点都位于一条直线上，在前一种情况下是直线，在后一种情况下是曲线。例如，仅考虑正数时，满足 $x+y=1$ 的坐标点位于图1中的直线 $AB$ 上，其中 $OA=1$ 且 $OB=1$。因此，直线段 $AB$ 提供了关系在限制为正数时的图像表现。

Another example of a relation between two variables is afforded[əˈfɔːrd ] by considering the variations[ˌverɪˈeʃən ] in the pressure[ˈpreʃər ] and volume[ˈvɑːljəm] of a given mass[mæs] of some gaseous[ˈɡæsiəs] substance[ˈsʌbstəns]—such as air or coal-gas or steam[stiːm]—at a constant[ˈkɑːnstənt ] temperature[ˈtemprətʃʊr ]. Let $v$ be the number of cubic[ ˈkjuːbɪk] feet in its volume and $p$ its pressure in lb. weight per square inch. Then the law, known as Boyle's law, expressing[ɪkˈspresɪŋ ] the relation between $p$ and $v$ as both vary[ ˈveri], is that the product $pv$ is constant， always supposing[ səˈpoʊzɪŋ ] that the temperature does not alter[ˈɔːltər]. Let us suppose, for example, that the quantity of the gas and its other circumstances[ˈsɜːrkəmstænsɪz] are such that we can put $pv=I$ (the exact number on the right-hand side of the equation makes no essential[ ɪˈsenʃ(ə)l ] difference).  

另一个关于两个变量之间关系的例子是，通过考虑一定质量的某种气体物质（如空气、煤气或蒸汽）在恒定温度下的压力和体积的变化来提供的。设$v$ 为其体积的立方英尺数，$p$ 为其压力，以每平方英寸磅数表示。然后，表达 $p$ 和$v$ 之间关系的定律，即波义耳定律，是说 $pv$ 的乘积是恒定的，前提是温度不变。让我们假设，例如，气体的量及其其他情况是这样的：我们可以设 $pv=I$（方程右侧的具体数值没有本质的区别）。

Then in Fig.2 we take two lines, $OV$ and $OP$ , at right angles and draw $OM$ along $OV$ to represent $v$ units of volume, and $ON$ along $OP$ to represent $p$ units of pressure. Then the point $Q$ ,which is found by completing the parallelogram[parallelogram ] $OMQN$ , represents the state of the gas when its volume is $v$ cubic[ˈkjuːbɪk] feet and its pressure id $p$ lb. weight per square inch. If the circumstances[ˈsɜːrkəmstænsɪz] of the portion[ˈpɔːrʃ(ə)n] of gas considered are such that $pv=I$, then all these points $Q$ which correspond[correspond ] to any possible state of this portion of gas must lie on the curved[curved ] line $ABC$ , which includes all points for which $p$ and $v$ are positive, and $pv=I$ . Thus this curved line gives a pictorial[pɪkˈtɔːriəl ] representation[ˌreprɪzenˈteɪʃ(ə)n] of the relation holding between the volume and the pressure. When the pressure is very big the corresponding[ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndɪŋ ] point $Q$ must be near $C$ , or even beyond $C$ on the undrawn part of the curve; then the volume will be very small. When the volume is big $Q$ will be near to $A$ n or beyond $A$ ; and then the pressure will be small. Notice that an engineer or a physicist may want to know the particular pressure corresponding[ ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːndɪŋ] to some definitely assigned[ əˈsaɪnd] volume[ ˈvɑːljəm]. Then we have the case of determining the $unknown$ $p$ when $v$ is a known number. But this is only in particular cases. In considering generally the properties of the gas and how it will behave[bɪˈheɪv], he has to have in his mind the general form of the whole curve $ABC$ and its general properties. 然后在图2中，我们取两条线，$OV$ 和 $OP$ ，垂直交叉，并沿 $OV$ 画出 $OM$ 代表 $v$ 个体积单位，沿 $OP$ 画出 $ON$ 代表 $p$ 个压力单位。然后，通过完成平行四边形 $OMQN$ 找到的点 $Q$ 表示了当气体体积为 $v$ 立方英尺，压力为每平方英寸 $p$ 磅时的气体状态。如果考虑到被分析气体部分的情况是 $pv=I$，那么所有这些点 $Q$，对应于该部分气体的任何可能状态，必须位于曲线 $ABC$ 上，该曲线包括所有 $p$ 和 $v$ 为正且 $pv=I$ 的点。因此，这条曲线提供了体积和压力之间关系的形象表示。当压力很大时，相应的点 $Q$ 必须接近 $C$，甚至在曲线未绘制部分之外；此时体积将非常小。当体积很大时，$Q$ 将接近 $A$ 或在 $A$ 之外；此时压力将很小。注意，工程师或物理学家可能想知道与某确定的体积对应的特定压力。那么，当 $v$ 是已知数时，我们需要确定未知的 $p$。但这只在特定情况下才会发生。在考虑气体的性质及其行为时，他必须心中有数整个曲线 $ABC$ 的一般形式及其一般特性。换句话说，真正基本的概念是满足关系式 $pv=I$ 的 $variables$ 对的概念。这个例子说明了在数学的应用和理论中，$variables$ 的概念是多么基础。
