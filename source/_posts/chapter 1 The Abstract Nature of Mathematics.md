## The Abstract Nature of Mathematics

**THE STUDY** of mathematics is apt to commence in disappointment. The important applications of the science, the theoretical interest of its ideas, and the logical rigour [ ˈrɪɡə(r)] of its methods, all generate the expectation of a speedy introduction to processes of interest. We are told that by its aid the stars are weighted and the billions of molecules[ˈmɒlɪkjuːlz] in a drop of water are counted. Yet, like the ghost of Hamlet's father, this great science eludes [ ɪˈluːd] the efforts of our mental weapons to grasp it — 'Tis here','tis there','tis gone'— and what we do see does not suggest the same excuse for illusiveness [ɪ'lu:sɪvnəs] as sufficed [səˈfaist] for the ghost , that  it is too noble for out gross methods. 'A show of violence [ˈvaɪələns] ,' if ever[ˈevə(r)] excusable[ɪkˈskjuːzəbl] , may surely be 'offered' to the trivial results which occupy [ˈɑkjupaɪ] the pages of some elementary [ˌelɪˈmentri] mathematical treatises['tri:tɪzɪz].
<!--more-->
数学的学习往往以失望开始。这门科学的重要应用、其思想的理论兴趣以及其方法的逻辑严谨，都引发了对迅速介入有趣过程的期望。我们被告知，借助它的帮助，我们可以衡量星星的重量，数出一滴水中的数十亿分子。然而，就像哈姆雷特的父亲的幽灵一样，这门伟大的科学逃避了我们精神武器的努力去理解它 —— ‘它在这儿’，‘它在那儿’，‘它又消失了’—— 而我们看到的东西并没有像幽灵那样，给出相同的借口，说明它是太崇高以至于超出了我们粗糙的方法。‘对暴力的展示’，如果曾经有理由的话，那一定是为了那些占据一些基础数学著作页面的琐碎结果。

​		The reason for this failure[ˈfeɪljə(r) ] of the science to live up to [达到实现] its reputation[ˌrepjuˈteɪʃ(ə)n] is that its fundamental ideas are not explained[ ɪkˈspleɪnd] to the student disentangled[disentangled] from the technical[ˈteknɪk(ə)l] procedure[ prəˈsiːdʒə(r) ] which has been invented to facilitate[ fəˈsɪlɪteɪt ] their exact presentation[ˌprez(ə)nˈteɪʃ(ə)n] in particular instances. Accordingly,the unfortunate[ʌnˈfɔːtʃənət] learner finds himself struggling to acquire[əˈkwaɪə(r) ] a knowledge of mass[ mæs] of details which are not illuminated[ɪˈluːmɪneɪtɪd] by any general conception. Without a doubt, technical facility[fəˈsɪləti] is a first requisite[ˈrekwɪzɪt] for valuable mental activity: we shall fail[feɪl ] to appreciate the rhythm of Milton, or the passion [ ˈpæʃ(ə)n] of Shelley, so long as we find it necessary to spell the words and are not quite certain of the forms of the individual[ˌɪndɪˈvɪdʒuəl] letters. In this sense[sens] there is no royal road to learning. But it is equally[ˈiːkwəli ] an error to confine[ kənˈfaɪn] attention to technical processes, excluding consideration of general ideas. Here lies the road to pedantry[ˈped(ə)ntri ].

这门科学未能实现其声誉的原因在于，其基本思想未能与为了在特定情况下准确呈现它们而创造的技术程序分开向学生解释。因此，不幸的学习者发现自己在努力获得大量细节的知识，而这些细节并没有被任何总体概念所阐明。毫无疑问，技术能力是有价值的心智活动的首要条件：只要我们发现有必要拼写单词，并且对单个字母的形式并不完全确定，我们就无法欣赏弥尔顿的韵律或雪莱的激情。从这个意义上说，没有通往学习的捷径。但同样错误的是，将注意力局限于技术过程，而不考虑总体思想。这里是走向书呆子的道路。

The object of the following chapters[ ˈtʃæptəz] is not to teach mathematics, but to enable students from the very beginning of their course[ kɔːs] to know what the science is about, and why it is necessarily the foundation of exact thought as applied[əˈplaɪd ] to natural phenomena[fəˈnɒmɪnə]. All allusion[ əˈluːʒ(ə)n] in what follows to detailed deductions [dɪˈdʌkʃnz] in any part of the science will be inserted merely[ˈmɪrli] for the purpose of example, and care will be taken to make the general argument comprehensible[ ˌkɑːmprɪˈhensəb(ə)l], even if here and there some technical process or symbol which the reader does not understand is cited[ ˈsaɪtɪd ] for the purpose of illustration[ˌɪləˈstreɪʃ(ə)n].

以下章节的目标不是教授数学，而是让学生从课程的一开始就了解这门科学的内容及其作用。即使在接下来的内容中提到了一些读者可能不理解的技术过程或符号，也仅仅是为了举例说明的目的，并且我们会确保整体论述易于理解。

The first acquaintance[əˈkweɪntəns] which most people have with mathematics is through[θruː] arithmetic[əˈrɪθmətɪk]. That two and two make four is usually taken as the type of a simple mathematical proposition[ˌprɒpəˈzɪʃ(ə)n] which everyone will have heard[hɜːd] of. Arithmetic, therefore, will be a good subject to consider in order to discover, if possible, the most obvious[ˈɑːbviəs] characteristic[ˌker.ək.təˈrɪs.tɪk ] of the science. Now, the first noticeable fact about arithmetic is that it applies[əˈplaɪz] to everything, to tastes and to sounds, to apples and to angels, to the ideas of the mind and to the bones[bəʊnz] of the body. The nature of the things[θɪŋz] is perfectly[ˈpɜːfɪktli] indifferent[ɪnˈdɪfrənt], of all things it is ture that two and two make four. Thus[ðʌs] we write down as the leading characteristic[ˌker.ək.təˈrɪs.tɪk ] of mathematics that it deals with properties and ideas which are applicable[əˈplɪkəb(ə)l] to things just because they are things, and apart from any particular feelings, or emotions, or sensations[ senˈseɪʃ(ə)n], in any way connected with them. This is what is meant by calling mathematics an abstract science.

大多数人对数学的第一次接触是通过算术。人们通常把两加二等于四视为一种简单的数学命题，每个人都会听说过。因此，算术将是一个很好的研究对象，以便尽可能地发现这门科学最显而易见的特征。现在，关于算术的第一个显而易见的事实是它适用于一切事物，无论是味觉、听觉、苹果、天使，还是心灵的思想和身体的骨骼。事物的本质是完全中立的，关于所有事物的事实是，两加二等于四。因此，我们将数学的主要特征写下来，即它处理适用于事物的属性和观念，仅仅因为它们是事物，而不受与它们有任何特定感受、情感或感觉有关的影响。这就是所谓的将数学称为抽象科学的含义。

The result which we have reached[riːtʃt] deserves[dɪˈzɜːrvz] attention[əˈtenʃ(ə)n]. It is natural to think that an abstract science cannot be of much importance in the affairs[əˈferz] of human life, because it has omitted[əˈmɪtɪd] from its consideration everything of real interest. It will be remembered that Swift, in his description of Gulliver's voyage to Laputa, is of two minds on this point. He describes the mathematicians[ ˌmæθəməˈtɪʃn] of that country as silly[ˈsɪli] and useless dreamers, whose attention has to be awakened[əˈweɪkənd] by flappers[ˈflæpər]. Also, the mathematical tailor[ˈteɪlər] measures his height by a quadrant[ˈkwɑːdrənt], and deduces[dɪˈduːsɪs] his other dimensions[ daɪˈmenʃ(ə)nz] by a rule and compasses[ˈkʌmpəsɪz], producing a suit of very ill-fitting[ˌɪl ˈfɪtɪŋ] clothes. On the other hand, the mathematicians of Laputa, by their marvelous[ˈmɑːrvələs] invention of the magnetic[mæɡˈnetɪk] island floating in the air, ruled the country and maintained[meɪnˈteɪnd] their ascendancy[əˈsendənsi] over their subject. Swift, indeed, lived at a time peculiarly[pɪˈkjuːliərli] unsuited[ʌnˈsuːtɪd] for gibes[dʒaɪbz] at contemporary[kənˈtempəreri ] mathematicians. Newton's Principia[prɪnˈsɪpɪəm] had just been written, one of the great forces[ˈfɔːrsɪz]which have transfomed the modern world. Swift might just as well have laughed at an earthquake.

我们所得出的结论值得关注。人们自然会认为，抽象科学在人类生活事务中并不是非常重要的，因为它在考虑时省略了所有真正感兴趣的事物。大家可能还记得，斯威夫特在描述格列佛的《拉普达之行》时在这一点上持有两种观点。他将那个国家的数学家描述为愚蠢无用的梦想家，需要用扇子唤醒他们的注意力。此外，数学裁缝用象限仪测量他的身高，然后用规则和圆规推算其他尺寸，做出了一套非常不合身的衣服。另一方面，拉普达的数学家们通过他们奇妙的发明——漂浮在空中的磁性岛屿，统治了这个国家，并保持了对他们的臣民的统治地位。事实上，斯威夫特生活在一个特别不适合嘲笑当时数学家的时代。牛顿的《自然哲学的数学原理》刚刚完成，这是一个改变现代世界的伟大力量之一。斯威夫特倒不如嘲笑一场地震。

But a mere[mɪr] list of the achievements of mathematics is an unsatisfactory[ˌʌnˌsætɪsˈfæktəri] way of arriving[əˈraɪvɪŋ] at an idea of its importance. It is worth while to spend a little thought in getting at the root reason why mathematics, because of it very abstractness, must always remain[rɪˈmeɪn] one of the most important topics for thought. Let us try to make clear to ourselves why explanations[ ˌekspləˈneɪʃənz] of the order of events necessarily[ˌnesəˈserəli ] tend to become mathematical.

然而，仅仅列举数学的成就是了解其重要性的不令人满意的方式。值得花一点时间思考，去找出数学因其非常抽象而始终保持着作为思考的最重要主题之一的根本原因。让我们尝试弄清楚为什么事件顺序的解释必然倾向于变得数学化。

Consider how all events are interconnected. When we see the lightning[ˈlaɪtnɪŋ], we listen for the thunder[ ˈθʌndər]; when we hear the wind[wɪnd], we look for the waves on the sea; in the chill[tʃɪl] autumn, the leaves fall. Everywhere order reigns[reɪnz], so that when some circumstances[ˈsɜːrkəmstænsɪz] have been noted[ˈnoʊtɪd] we can foresee[ fɔːrˈsiː] that others will also be present[ˈprez(ə)nt]. The progress[ ˈprɑːɡres]of science consists[kənˈsɪsts] in observing there interconnection and in showing with a patient[ ˈpeɪʃ(ə)nt] ingenuity[ˌɪndʒəˈnuːəti] that the events of this ever-shifting world are but examples of a few general connections or relations called laws. To see what is general in what is particular and what is permanent[ ˈpɜːrmənənt] in what is transitory[ˈtrænzətɔːri] is the aim of scientific[ ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk] thought. In the eyes of science, the fall of an apple, the motion[ˈmoʊʃ(ə)n] of a planet round a sun, and the clinging[ ˈklɪŋɪŋ] of the atmosphere[ ˈætməsfɪr] to the earth are all seen as examples of the laws of gravity[ˈɡrævəti]. This possibility[ˌpɑːsəˈbɪləti ] if disentangling[ ˌdɪsɪnˈtæŋɡ(ə)l] the most complex evanescent[ ˌevəˈnes(ə)nt] circumstances[ˈsɜːrkəmstænsɪz] into various examples of permanent[ˈpɜːrmənənt] laws is the controlling idea of modern though.

考虑一下所有事件是如何相互关联的。当我们看到闪电时，我们会听雷声；当我们听到风声时，我们会看海上的波浪；在寒冷的秋天，树叶落下。到处都有秩序存在，因此当某些情况被注意到时，我们可以预见到其他情况也会出现。科学的进步在于观察它们之间的相互关联，并展示出一种耐心的创造力，即这个不断变化的世界中的事件只是几个称为定律的一般关联或关系的例子。看到特殊中的普遍，以及暂时中的永久，是科学思维的目标。在科学眼中，苹果的落下、行星绕太阳运动，以及大气对地球的附着，都被视为重力定律的例子。将最复杂、最短暂的情况分解成各种永久定律的例子，这种可能性是现代思想的控制理念。

Now let us think of  the sort of laws which we want in order completely to realize this scientific ideal. Our knowledge of the particular facts of the world around us is gained from our sensations[senˈseɪʃ(ə)n].  We see, and hear, and taste, and smell, and feel hot and cold, and push, and rub[rub], and ache[eɪk], and tingle[ˈtɪŋɡl ]. These are just our own personal sensations[senˈseɪʃ(ə)n]: my toothache[ˈtuːθeɪk] cannot be your toothache, and my sight cannot be your sight[saɪt ]. But we ascribe[ əˈskraɪb] the origin[ˈɔːrɪdʒɪn ] of these sensations[senˈseɪʃ(ə)n] to relations between the things which form the external world. Thus[ðʌs] the dentist[ˈdentɪst] extracts[ˈekstræktz] not the toothache but the tooth. And not only so, we also endeavour[ ɪnˈdevər] to imagine[ɪˈmædʒɪn] the world as one connected set of things which underlies[ˌʌndərˈlaɪz] all the perceptions[pərˈsepʃən] of all people. There is not one world of things for my sensations and another for yours , but one world in which we both exist. It is the same tooth both for dentist and patient. Also we hear and we touch the same world as we see.

现在让我们考虑一下，为了完全实现这一科学理想，我们需要什么样的法则。我们对周围世界的具体事实的认识来自于我们的感觉。我们看到、听到、尝到、闻到、感受热和冷，推、摩擦、疼痛、刺痛。这些只是我们自己的个人感觉：我的牙痛不可能是你的牙痛，我的视觉也不可能是你的视觉。但我们将这些感觉的起源归因于构成外部世界的事物之间的关系。因此，牙医取出的不是牙痛，而是牙齿。而且不仅如此，我们还努力想象世界是一个连接的一套事物，它支撑着所有人的所有感知。我的感觉中没有一个世界，你的感觉中另一个世界，而是一个我们两者都存在的世界。对于牙医和患者来说，是同一颗牙齿。我们听到的、触摸到的，和我们看到的是同一个世界。

It is easy, therefore, to understand that we want to describe the connexions between these external things in some way which does not depend on any particular sensations ,nor even on all the sensations of any particular person. The laws satisfied[ˈsætɪsfaɪd ] by the course[kɔːrs] of events in the world of external[ɪkˈstɜːrn(ə)l ] things are to be described, if possible, in a neutral[ˈnuːtrəl] universal[ˌjuːnɪˈvɜːrs(ə)l] fashion[ˈfæʃ(ə)n, the same for blind[ blaɪnd] men as for deaf[def] men, and the same for beings with faculties[ˈfækltiz] beyond our ken[ken] as for normal human beings.

因此，很容易理解我们想要以某种方式描述这些外部事物之间的联系，这种方式不依赖于任何特定的感觉，甚至不依赖于任何特定个人的所有感觉。在外部事物世界中事件的发展过程中所满足的法则，如果可能的话，应以一种中立、普遍的方式描述，这种方式对盲人和聋人来说是相同的，对那些具有超出我们理解的能力的存在来说也是相同的，就像对普通人类一样。

But when we have put aside[ əˈsaɪd] our immediate sensations, the most serviceable[ ˈsɜːrvɪsəb(ə)l] part—from its clearness[ˈklɪrnəs], definiteness[de'fɪnətnes], and universality[ˌjuːnɪvɜːrˈsæləti] —of what is left is composed[kəmˈpoʊzd] of our general ideas of the abstract formal[ˈfɔːrm(ə)l ] properties of things; in fact, the abstract mathematical ideas mentioned[ˈmenʃ(ə)nd] above. Thus it comes about that, step by step, and not realizing[ ˈriːəlaɪzɪŋ] the full meaning of the process, mankind[mænˈkaɪnd ] has been led to search for a mathematical description of the properties of the universe[ ˈjuːnɪvɜːrs ], because in this way only can a general idea of the course of events be formed , freed from reference[ˈrefrəns ] to particular persons or to particular types of sensation. For example, it might be asked at dinner:'What was it which underlay[ˈʌndərleɪ] my sensation of sight, yours of touch [tʌtʃ], and his of taste and smell?' the answer being 'an apple'. But in its final[ ˈfaɪn(ə)l ] analysis[əˈnæləsɪs], science seeks[ siːk] to describe an apple in terms of the positions and motions of molecules[ˈmɑːləˌkjuːlz] , a description which ignores me and you and him , and also ignores sight and touch and taste and smell. Thus mathematical ideas , because they are abstract, supply[ səˈplaɪ] just what is wanted for a scientific[ˌsaɪənˈtɪfɪk ] description of the course of events.

当我们抛开了我们的直接感觉时，剩下的最实用部分——因其清晰、明确和普遍适用——是由我们对事物的抽象形式特性的一般观念组成的；实际上，就是上文提到的抽象数学思想。因此，逐步地，而且并未意识到这个过程的全部含义，人类被引导去寻求对宇宙特性的数学描述，因为只有通过这种方式，才能形成对事件发展过程的一般概念，摆脱对特定个人或特定感觉类型的参考。例如，在晚餐时可能会被问到：“是什么构成了我的视觉感知，你的触觉感知，以及他的味觉和嗅觉？”答案是“一个苹果”。但在最终的分析中，科学试图以分子的位置和运动来描述一个苹果，这种描述忽略了我、你和他，也忽略了视觉、触觉、味觉和嗅觉。因此，数学思想因其抽象性，提供了科学描述事件过程所需的内容。

This point has usually been misunderstood, from being thought of in too narrow[ˈnæroʊ ] a way. Pythagoras[ pəˈθægərəs ] had a glimpse[ ɡlɪmps] of it when he proclaimed[ prəˈkleɪmd] that number was the source of all things. In modern times the belief that the ultimate[ˈʌltɪmət ] explanation of all things was to be found in Newtonian mechanics[məˈkænɪks ] was an adumbration[ˌædʌmˈbreɪʃn] of the truth that all science as it grows towards perfection becomes mathematical[ ˌmæθəˈmætɪk(ə)l ] in its ideas.

这一观点通常被误解，因为人们将其看得太狭隘。当毕达哥拉斯宣称数字是一切事物的源头时，他曾经对此有所感悟。在现代，人们相信一切事物的最终解释可以在牛顿力学中找到，这预示了一个事实，即随着科学不断向完美迈进，所有科学都变得数学化。
